# Methodology & Risk Disclosure — [EFFECTIVE DATE]

This document describes, at a high level, how **[APP NAME]** produces Decision Outputs and what limitations apply.

## 1) What the App does
The App helps users think through tradeoffs between Original Medicare + Medigap (Medicare Supplement), Medicare Advantage, and related choices such as Part D and interaction with Medicaid/LIS (where relevant). The App focuses on risk management tradeoffs: premium predictability, provider flexibility, administrative friction, and potential out‑of‑pocket exposure.

## 2) What the App does NOT do
The App does not enroll users, recommend a specific insurer/carrier, guarantee eligibility, pricing, underwriting outcomes, or plan availability, provide medical advice, or connect to Medicare.gov accounts or retrieve claims data (no Blue Button integration in the current version).

## 3) Inputs
Decision Outputs are driven by user‑supplied inputs and may use defaults where inputs are incomplete. The App may display “low confidence” where uncertainty is high.

## 4) Scoring and scenario modeling (conceptual)
The App may compute comparative scores based on weighted factors such as utilization scenarios, financial risk exposure, switching/underwriting asymmetry risk, network dependence, prescription risk, and benefit volatility. It may also simulate “best/median/worst” scenarios for sensitivity.

## 5) Known limitations
The App may use public sources and generalized assumptions. Plan details and rules change. Real‑world decisions involve personal values and circumstances that no model fully captures. Users should verify important details through authoritative sources.
