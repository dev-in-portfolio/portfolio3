Before you use this

Not advice

This is a decision-support tool — not medical, legal, financial, or benefits advice. It produces an educational comparison based on the information you provide and simplified rule heuristics.

Not enrollment. Not sales.

This app does not sell plans, does not take commissions, does not recommend specific carriers, and does not enroll you in Medicare, Medicare Advantage, Part D, or Medigap.

Verify with official sources

Medicare rules, state Medigap protections, provider networks, formularies, and premiums change. Always confirm enrollment periods, eligibility, and plan details with Medicare, your state SHIP office, and/or the plan itself.

Privacy & sharing

By default, this app stores progress in your device’s browser storage so you can resume. If you export or share a link, you may be copying sensitive personal information (including health-related answers). Use the "Safe" export/share option if you want redaction.

What the engine does

  • Scores three structures: Medigap, MA PPO, MA HMO
  • Models cost predictability vs. worst-case risk
  • Models provider/network dependency
  • Models administrative friction (authorizations, referrals, denials)
  • Warns about potential Medigap underwriting lockouts

Case Study — Coverage Compass

Short skim first. Then the medium story. Deep dive if you want the mechanics.

Skim (30–45 sec)

  • Broken frame: Most tools treat Medicare choice like shopping.
  • Why it fails: This is risk navigation across time with asymmetric consequences.
  • Reframe: "What happens if I'm wrong?" beats "what looks best today?"
  • System: A decision environment that surfaces tradeoffs + uncertainty + top drivers.
  • Signal: I build explainable, failure-aware systems that don’t pretend certainty.

Medium (2–3 min)

Most Medicare decision tools simplify the wrong thing. They optimize for what’s easy to compare (premium, perks) and hide what’s expensive to misunderstand (lock-in, switching asymmetry, downside risk).

Coverage Compass reframes the decision as constrained transitions under uncertainty. The UI is designed to show consequences, not just options — including what would change the recommendation and why.

The core design principle: treat failure modes as first-class UX. If the model can be wrong, the user should see where and how.
